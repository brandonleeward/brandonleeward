## The Arc

AI found me before I understood what it was.

I was using these tools daily and learning faster, thinking through 
problems differently, growing in ways that genuinely surprised me. 
The models were pulling me forward. It was empowering. 

I've been doing evaluation work as a means to earn income in addition
to editing work. Scoring outputs, following rubrics, advancing through
quality review roles across multiple platforms. Something to pay the 
bills, at least I thought. 

But somewhere in that work something shifted. I started understanding 
what was actually happening underneath. BlueDot Impact's Future of AI 
course opened up the alignment and safety landscape. I started reading 
seriously - Russell, the technical literature, the philosophical 
arguments. I started to see that every scored output, every piece of 
feedback, was human judgment being written into a model.

That wasn't just a gig anymore. That was participation in something 
consequential.

And I wanted more of it. Not just assessing from the outside but 
shaping from the inside. Building the systems directly. Developing 
real engineering skills that could make a genuine impact on how these 
models work and what they become.

So I started building. Seriously. With no plans to stop.
